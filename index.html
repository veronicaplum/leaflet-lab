Inbound Tourism in China (2007-2015)

China opened its boarders in 1978 at the beginning of its economic reform. This was only the start of the Westernization, economic boost and not so far off, important role that the nation would play globally across the board.

This movement forever changed modern Chinese culture and booming tourism industry. Thousands of expats live in China today and there definately a micro-culture surrounding that spatiality. In gerenal the industry is shifting today. Previously, the terracotta warriors in Xi'an and the Great Wall near Beijing were popular places and still are. Except in recent years, the visitor toll on the Great Wall has been lower in comparison, where more tourists appear to prefer methods of ecotourism. Yet this freedom comes at a price, while many foreigners experience troubles with the Chinese government surrounding nitpicky things, such as having an approved place of residence every night during their stay in china. In short, no matter where a forieigner chooses to stay, they have to registered and documented there.

In an alterntaive, outbound context, China itself has a Passport Power Rank of 44. With the United States and United Kingdom at Rank 1, and South Sudan and Myanmar at the bottom rank of 80. But there are quickly becomeing more and more Chinese tourist arund the world.
